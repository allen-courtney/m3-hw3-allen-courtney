My Favorite Artist: Gustav Klimt

About Gustav Klimt

Gustav Klimt is an Austraian painter known for his highly decorative style and erotic nature of his works, which were seen as a rebellion against the traditional academic art of his time. His most famous paitings are The Kiss and Potrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer.

Klimt was born in 1862 in Baumgarten, Austria, not far from Vienna. At 14, Klimt enrolled in Vienna's School of Applied Arts where he studied a range of subjects, including fresco painting and mosaic. Klimt would share that his artwork inspiration came from his predecessor Hans Makart and by the Viennese avent-garde, then later Klimt's inspiration became the psychological inquiry and preoccupation with sexuality that pervaded the Viennese avant-garde. Women had always been Klimt's favorite subjects, "I am less interested in myself as a subject for paiting than I am in other people, above all women," he once said.

Klimt would go on to create beautiful artwork over the years and pushing artistic boundaries. He later died at age 55, from a stroke.
